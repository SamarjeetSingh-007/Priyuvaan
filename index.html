  • What are SEBI requirements for SME IPO in India?
    Companies need minimum ₹10 crore post-issue paid-up capital, 3 years operational track record, and SEBI compliance. We guide you through NSE Emerge and BSE SME platform requirements.
  • How much capital can SMEs raise through Indian markets?
    SME platforms allow raising up to ₹200 crores with minimum issue size of ₹10 crores. Our clients have successfully raised between ₹15-75 crores through NSE Emerge and BSE SME platforms.
  • What is the typical timeline for SME IPO listing in India?
    The complete process takes 6-9 months from initial assessment to NSE/BSE listing, including due diligence, SEBI filing, regulatory approvals, and market opening. We ensure efficient timeline management.
  • What are the cost implications for SME IPO in India?
    Total costs range from ₹35-50 lakhs including merchant banking fees, legal expenses, compliance costs, and listing fees. We offer competitive fee structures aligned with issue size and complexity.
